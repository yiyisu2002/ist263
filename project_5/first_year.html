First Year in SU

SummerStart | Fall 2022 | Spring 2023 | Course Summary

JULY 2022 - AUGUST 2022 SUMMERSTART

Attended the SummerStart Program, in the first day of the SummerStart, I walked around the campus, curious about everything there in SU.

Class began, I took Psychology and English, that was the first time for me to handle college course, I was a bit stress. Fortunately, I found a way to relieve my stress—eating while working.

One week later, I received some gifts from Newhouse Visitor Center. Meanwhile, I was adopted to my study life so far.

In following weeks I made some friends and we began our daily activities of play badminton in the Barnes Center after class and play Mahjong before sleep.

Last few weeks of the summer program, I saw the most beautiful sunset I ever seem through the window in Ernie Davis Hall.

At the end of the program, me and my friends had a small celebration. That was the last time all of us stay together.

AUGUST 2022 - DECEMBER 2022 FALL SEMESTER

Settled down in Day Hall, this is the tidiest time this dorm has ever been in this academic year. My roommate was nice, the split-double allows as to talk to each other meanwhile keep privacies.

Opening Ceremony for SU first-year student. Go Orange!

Class begins, I chose 18 credits of classes, that makes me busier than summer and occasionally anxious. Well…I did choose Japanese class, but I wasn’t expected that gonna kill me, I hate learning language (and I guess that is the reason that my English is still not that good)! Btw the ice cream was good.

Got my first job in the college from Prof. Rubin, work as a student gate crew, getting paid and watching football game.

The first-time attending career fair! I was kind of lost, I didn’t even prepare my resume, but I was thought that should be fine since that was only my first semester here. I never attend such an event before, so I was curious about what that going to be.

Attended Google’s workshop, it was great and useful, but I don’t have any background they are looking for. Fine, I still got time.

As the weather getting colder, me and my roommate decide to have a little hotpot (which was not allowed in dorm but... you know, college students never follow the rule *just kidding).

A poor kid struggling on her calculus homework, and that is part of my daily life. However, I did great in my quizzes and exams, I am still don’t understand why the homework was so difficult.

Me and friends rented a car and drive to Sliver River. The three of us stayed in the car for a night with shinning stars, and went to the national park near by Rochester the next day. A good chance to escape from course work and breath in the nature.

Went to ESF that next to SU, saw some amazing specimens, plants, and computer collections. I was surprised that there were so many treasures in this school.

The first snow in my college journey!

Thanksgiving break in Florida with other three friends from SummerStart. We went to Miami, Orlando, and Tampa in a week, that was a bit rush but still got some fun. Everything was great except the weather (lol).

JANUARY 2023 - APRIL 2023 SPRING SEMESTER

New semester but keep studying calculus…and now there is another monster comes together—Python! It was very usual for me to debug for hours, there was once that I was sit in front of my laptop and debugging until 5am in the morning.

Chinese New Year Party in a friend’s apartment! That was tough to celebrate because there is no break in the US for the day, but we did our best to create a festival atmosphere.

Received the emails of entered on the Dean’s List for the Fall Semester in both Newhouse and iSchool. That is such a milestone that showing I am adopted into college life successfully. A few days later, I had been invited to attend the Success Scholar event.

Valentine’s Day Activity that hosted by WeMedia Lab, which hided 52 roses (represents “love you” in Mandarin). I found one in Newhouse! Also, the dinning hall has heart donut in the same day.

Participated in Newhouse Leader in Communication Event, watched the interview of Chief Transformation Officer, Stacey Tank, one of our alumni’s speech about her experience. Got some valuable insights about communication.

Spring break in Boston and Washington DC, when flowers and cherry blossoms begins to bloom. The temperature was not that friendly to outdoor activities but who cares!

Participated in The Alexia Presents event hosted by Newhouse, zoom with two wonderful short video directors who won Academy Award. The host was one of the people who attended the award ceremony, that was such a special experience to see someone in real life that you were thought you can never get that close to him.

Adopted three succulents from Newhouse on the national plant day! Thanks to Visitor Center, the office in Newhouse that always trying to provide a colorful student life in Newhouse.

Beautiful sunset, but I forgot my online exam of my Python class that day.

Received a white hair at the end of the semester…

END of the First Year
